About

Hello! I am a former auditor-turned-consultant-turned-software engineer. My journey into the industry was fueled by my love for problem-solving and an eagerness for learning new things. What excites me most about tech is its constant evolution– there’s always a new skill or language to pick up. Sometimes the challenge lies in deciding where to focus your efforts. I’ve discovered a passion for taking an idea or vision and collaborating with other engineers to bring it to life through code. I find myself drawn to the creative side of software development, where I can merge my interest in graphic design and coding to create visually appealing and intuitive applications. For me, ensuring a seamless user experience is just as important as the functionality of the product, as it plays a critical role in user adoption and satisfaction. As a full-stack engineer specializing in JavaScript, I’ve built several applications that reflect this approach. I strive to balance functionality with design to deliver the best possible product.

Career Journey

Before diving into software engineering, I studied accounting in college, earned my MBA, and started my career as an auditor for a large public accounting firm. My first major project involved auditing the Department of Defense, where I traveled to different bases around the world to ensure that inventory records reported by the DoD actually matched what was held at the base– a challenging task that involved a lot of attention to detail and problem-solving skills. After that, I transitioned to my consulting role at Deloitte. I advised clients on how to navigate audits, helping them resolve issues and prepare for future evaluations. Although I eventually realized that accounting wasn’t my true passion, I discovered a love for problem-solving and helping clients turn abstract ideas into actionable solutions. This problem-solving drive led me to software development, where I could leverage my analytical skills and technical expertise in a more creative and impactful way. The skills gained as an auditor and consultant– analytical thinking, adaptability, and collaboration– have been invaluable in my transition to software engineering. My experience has equipped me with a unique perspective, enabling me to approach development with both precision and creativity. I thrive in collaborative environments, where I can contribute to producing polished, user-centric products that solve real-world problems.

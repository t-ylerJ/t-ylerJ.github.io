Career Journey

Before my transition into data analysis, I studied accounting in college, earned my MBA, and started my career as an auditor for a large public accounting firm (KPMG). My first major project involved auditing the Department of Defense, where I traveled to different bases around the world to ensure that inventory records reported by the DoD actually matched what was held at the base– a challenging task that involved a lot of attention to detail and problem-solving skills. After that, I transitioned to my consulting role at Deloitte. I advised clients on how to navigate audits, helping them resolve issues and prepare for future evaluations. Although I eventually realized that accounting wasn’t my true passion, I discovered a love for problem-solving and helping clients turn abstract ideas into actionable solutions. This problem-solving drive led me to explore data analysis, where I could leverage my analytical skills and technical expertise in a more creative and impactful way. Wanting to build more technical solutions, I enrolled in a full-time coding bootcamp, where I gained hands-on experience in software development using JavaScript and SQL. It wasn’t until after the bootcamp, while applying to jobs, that I discovered Business Intelligence—and everything clicked. BI brought together my business background, technical training, and passion for data in a way that felt like a natural next step. Today, as a BI Specialist, I design and implement data-driven solutions that empower better decision-making. I write SQL to extract, clean, and transform large datasets, automate workflows with Python to boost efficiency, and build interactive dashboards in Power BI to surface meaningful insights. Whether it's optimizing a reporting process or building a new analytics tool from scratch, I aim to make data more accessible, accurate, and actionable. Data Analysis has become the sweet spot of my career—it allows me to blend strategic thinking with technical execution. I’m especially energized by opportunities to work cross-functionally, solve real business problems with data, and continuously refine how teams interact with and leverage information. I’m excited to keep growing in the data space, particularly in roles that combine analysis, automation, and collaboration to drive meaningful outcomes. The skills gained as an auditor and consultant– analytical thinking, adaptability, and collaboration– have been invaluable in my transition to data analytics.
